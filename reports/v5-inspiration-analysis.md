## Pattern Notes

| Site | Useful V5 Lesson | What To Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Apple | Lead with product presence, large simple headlines, roomy sections, and emotional product framing before details. | Do not mimic Apple hardware-grid layouts or overuse generic product cards. |
| Flighty | Make a utility feel desirable by turning real-world stress into a crisp emotional promise: know sooner, feel calmer, travel better. | Do not invent awards, ratings, or travel data claims for Let’s Build Apps. |
| Arc | Use personality, calm copy, and product screenshots to make software feel human and opinionated. | Do not import public social proof or testimonials. |
| Linear | Strong hierarchy, dark/light contrast, precise product screenshots, and restrained motion make complex software feel premium. | Avoid enterprise/productivity language that does not fit family apps. |
| Raycast | A short, memorable hero can carry the whole page when backed by motion, interaction, and visible product surfaces. | Avoid fake community numbers or celebrity trust signals. |
| Notion | A family of features can feel unified when each section shares a consistent visual grammar. | Avoid dense workspace/dashboard framing. |
| Things | Calm, focused lifestyle software can feel premium through whitespace, clarity, and a strong sense of craft. | Avoid productivity-bro language for Good Habits. |
| Craft | Documents/productivity tools become more inviting when screenshots are staged like tactile objects, not flat thumbnails. | Avoid heavy decorative chrome that competes with screenshots. |
| Superhuman | Conversion copy works when it names an emotional outcome, then supports it with a direct CTA. | Do not borrow unsupported speed/time-saving claims. |

## V5 Design Decisions

- Build a separate `v5/` experience so V4 production remains untouched.
- Make screenshots the first visual language: floating iPhone frames, layered product surfaces, and product-specific launch sections.
- Use concise emotional copy instead of broad app descriptions.
- Keep CTAs honest: App Store where verified, TestFlight interest where no public invite exists.
- Use tasteful CSS-only motion: reveal on scroll, hover lift, floating hero devices, and reduced-motion support.
- Keep the visual language connected through shared typography, rounded device frames, warm neutrals, ink text, amber highlights, soft lilac/green accents, and product-specific section tints.
- Avoid fabricated trust signals. Trust comes from real screenshots, verified App Store availability, privacy-first copy, and clear in-development status.

## Homepage Story Arc

1. Brand promise: beautiful tools for everyday family life.
2. Immediate proof: product screenshots in floating iPhone mockups.
3. Product launch sections: each app gets one emotional job and one clear CTA.
4. Philosophy: built for real life, privacy-first, focused apps.
5. Final conversion: choose the app that matches today’s need.
