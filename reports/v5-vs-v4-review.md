## Comparison

| Category | V4 | V5 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Visual impact | Faithful Stitch conversion with dashboard/app-collection feel. Clear and consistent, but more informational than emotional. | Large product-led hero, floating iPhone screenshots, deeper hierarchy, and launch-style sections create stronger first impression. |
| Clarity | Strong at listing apps and routes. Users can quickly see what exists. | Stronger at explaining why each app matters through one emotional promise per product. |
| Conversion potential | App CTAs are present, but the page behaves more like an index. | CTAs sit beside product screenshots and emotional framing, which should make App Store/TestFlight actions more compelling. |
| Mobile experience | Stable and usable. Screens read like stacked cards. | More immersive on first load, with hero copy and product mockups visible in the opening mobile viewport. |
| Product desirability | Accurate and tidy, but restrained. | More premium and memorable, especially for Countdowns, Sentences, and Perfect Coffee. |
| Trust | Avoids fake social proof after V4 cleanup. | Keeps trust honest with real screenshots, verified App Store links, privacy-first copy, and clear in-development labels. |
| Risk | Low risk because it is production-proven and already deployed. | Higher creative risk because it is a new direction; should remain preview-only until reviewed on real devices. |

## Recommendation

Keep V4 live while V5 is reviewed as a preview. V5 is a stronger candidate for a future homepage because it gives the product family more emotional weight, but it should go through real-device QA and copy review before replacing production.
